Brain Health

Protect the Brain You Can't Replace

Cognitive decline doesn't begin at 70. The biological processes that lead to Alzheimer's and other neurodegenerative conditions typically begin 15–20 years before symptoms appear. The best time to intervene is now — while you're sharp, and the window for meaningful prevention is still wide open.

At Impact Medical, our cognitive health program takes a longevity-first approach to brain health — combining neurological risk assessment, advanced biomarker testing, and lifestyle-based interventions to protect and enhance your cognitive function over the long term.

This isn't about treating dementia. It's about never getting there.

Why It Matters

The Prevention Window Is Earlier Than You Think

Amyloid plaques — the hallmark of Alzheimer's disease — can begin accumulating in the brain 20 years before a diagnosis. By the time memory symptoms appear, significant neurological damage has already occurred.

Modern medicine now has the tools to identify elevated risk early — and proven lifestyle, hormonal, and metabolic interventions that meaningfully reduce it. The question is whether you act when intervention can still make a difference.

Our Approach

What We Evaluate & Treat

Cognitive Assessment & Baseline Testing

Establishing your cognitive baseline is the first step — creating a benchmark to track change over time and identify early signs of decline before they become clinically significant.

Neurocognitive performance testing
Memory, processing speed, and executive function assessment
Baseline documentation for longitudinal tracking

Genetic & Biomarker Risk Assessment

Understanding your genetic predisposition and current biological markers gives us the clearest picture of your individual risk — and where to focus prevention efforts.

APOE genotyping (Alzheimer's genetic risk)
Inflammatory brain health markers
Homocysteine, B12, folate — neurological health markers
Blood sugar and insulin — metabolic drivers of cognitive decline

Sleep & Brain Recovery

Sleep is the brain's primary mechanism for clearing toxic waste products — including amyloid. Chronic poor sleep is one of the most significant and underappreciated risk factors for cognitive decline.

Advanced sleep evaluation and sleep architecture assessment
Sleep apnea screening and management
Sleep optimization protocols and interventions

Lifestyle & Prevention Protocol

The most powerful brain health interventions are lifestyle-based. We build a personalized protocol that targets the specific drivers of your individual risk — not a generic wellness checklist.

Exercise prescription for neurogenesis and brain health
Nutritional strategy for cognitive protection
Stress management and mental resilience
Evidence-based supplementation (omega-3, vitamin D, B-complex, etc.)
